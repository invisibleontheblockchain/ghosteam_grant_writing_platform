# Sober AF Entertainment (SAFE) Grant Writing Knowledge Base

## Organization Overview

### Mission Statement
Sober AF Entertainment's mission is to create safe and inclusive environments for individuals in recovery and those who choose to live a sober lifestyle. By promoting and hosting sober sections at mainstream events, we aim to reduce the stigma associated with sobriety and offer a supportive community for those seeking connection without the influence of substances. Beyond events, SAFE is dedicated to providing resources and education centered on peer support and harm-reduction initiatives. We empower individuals to celebrate life and enjoy memorable experiences without the need for alcohol or drugs. Our commitment extends to bridging the gap between the sober community and the general public, fostering understanding, acceptance, and a culture of inclusivity.

### History
- Founded in 2018 by a group of passionate individuals who recognized a significant gap in the entertainment industry: the lack of sober spaces at mainstream events
- Started as small gatherings, quickly gained traction leading to partnerships with major event organizers and venues across the country
- More than just an event organizer - advocates for cultural shift, challenging societal norms that equate fun and relaxation with substance use

### Core Programs

#### 1. Sober Sections at Mainstream Events
- Partners with major event organizers and venues to designate sober sections at concerts, sports events, and festivals
- Provides substance-free environment allowing attendees to enjoy events without pressures or triggers of alcohol and drugs

#### 2. Harm Reduction Workshops & Education
- Organizes workshops educating community about harm reduction strategies, safe substance use, and overdose prevention
- Distributes naloxone kits and provides training on their use
- Partners with CDPHE for bulk naloxone distribution

#### 3. Peer Support Specialist Training
- COPA-approved training program for Peer Recovery Specialists
- Trains individuals in long-term recovery to become peer mentors
- Provides guidance, support, and community to those navigating challenges of early recovery
- Students complete 500-hour training requirement

## Program Models by Location

### MSU Denver Partnership (Caring 4 Denver Grant)
- **Duration**: 3 years
- **Total Budget**: $119,706 ($39,902/year)
- **Events**: 8 per year (24 total)
- **Target Attendance**: 2400 over 3 years (100 per event average)
- **Key Partner**: Auraria Recovery Community (ARC)
- **Unique Elements**: 
  - Graduate intern from ARC
  - 2 Event Leads per event
  - 4 Peer Care Coordinators per event
  - 1 Faculty Opioid Overdose Response Trainer

### Colorado Mesa University Program (MESA County)
- **Duration**: 2 years
- **Total Budget**: $15,400 ($7,700/year)
- **Events**: 4 per year (2 football tailgates, 2 student events)
- **Target Attendance**: 200 per year
- **Unique Elements**: Smaller scale but similar structure to MSU Denver

### CU Boulder "You Got A Peer In Me" (Boulder HEF)
- **Duration**: 1 year
- **Total Budget**: $117,044
- **Events**: 8 community-led events
- **Target Attendance**: 2400 students
- **Unique Elements**:
  - Hire 2 student interns for program management
  - Certify 20-30 CU Boulder students as peer support specialists
  - QR code-based data collection system
  - Focus on expanding existing CRC

### University of Northern Colorado (Weld County)
- **Events**: 16 total (8 on-campus + 8 at Greeley Stampede)
- **Unique Elements**:
  - Partnership with Centennial Peaks Hospital
  - Community events at Greeley Stampede
  - Train 10-20 students as Peer Recovery Specialists

### Boulder County Opioid Settlement
- **Total Budget**: $195,096
- **Focus**: Recovery services with emphasis on peer support
- **Target**: 20 peer specialists trained annually
- **Service Areas**: Boulder and Longmont

- **Duration**: 3-5 hours per event (tailgates include pre-game, game, post-game)
- **Components**:
  - Sober social activities (music, games, food)
  - Peer support services
  - Harm reduction education
  - Naloxone distribution and training
  - Resource distribution
  - Real-time feedback collection

### Peer Support Training
- COPA-approved curriculum
- 500-hour training requirement
- Paid positions for peer specialists
- Focus on lived experience
- Cultural competency training included

### Proposal Structure
1. **Need Statement**: Focus on college substance use statistics, lack of sober spaces, mental health challenges
2. **Program Description**: Detailed activities, timeline, partnerships
3. **Outcomes**: Both quantitative (attendance, kits distributed) and qualitative (satisfaction, knowledge gain)
4. **Evaluation**: Comprehensive data collection and continuous improvement
5. **Sustainability**: Diverse funding, community partnerships, student leadership

### Common Outcomes Cited
- Increased awareness and education about substance misuse risks
- Enhanced peer support networks
- Improved mental and behavioral health
- Reduced substance-related incidents
- Strengthened community ties
- Positive feedback and engagement
- Program sustainability and expansion
