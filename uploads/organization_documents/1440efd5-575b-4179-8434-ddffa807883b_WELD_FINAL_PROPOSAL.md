## COVER PAGE - Executive Summary (250 words max)

Sober AF Entertainment (SAFE) proposes the "You Got A Peer In Me" UNCo Expansion Program to address Weld County's opioid crisis through comprehensive collegiate recovery support, community engagement, and peer workforce development. Building on SAFE's proven track record of training 240+ peer specialists across Colorado, this initiative establishes a robust Collegiate Recovery Community (CRC) at the University of Northern Colorado while extending recovery support throughout Weld County.

Our multi-faceted approach includes:

**1. CRC Development:** Establish and grow UNCo's CRC, providing critical recovery infrastructure for students navigating substance use challenges while pursuing academic goals.

**2. Campus & Community Events:** Execute 12 evidence-based events (6 on-campus, 6 at Greeley Stampede) reaching 2,400+ individuals with peer support, harm reduction education, and sober social opportunities.

**3. Peer Workforce Development:** Certify 30 peer specialists (20 English, 10 Spanish) through COPA-approved training, creating sustainable bilingual recovery support infrastructure.

**4. Harm Reduction:** Distribute 100+ naloxone kits with comprehensive training, directly preventing opioid overdoses in high-risk settings.

This program addresses critical gaps identified in Weld County's opioid response: limited collegiate recovery resources, insufficient peer support specialists, and disconnected recovery services. By leveraging SAFE's expertise, established partnership with Centennial Area Health Education Center (CAHEC), and proven event models including Greeley Stampede and Colorado Eagles games, we will create lasting change in how Weld County addresses substance use among young adults.

Our data-driven approach ensures accountability with measurable outcomes including CRC participation rates, event attendance, peer certifications, and overdose reversals tracked through our comprehensive evaluation system.

#### Question 2: Where applicable, describe any challenges or barriers that your entity has experienced in making progress towards these key strategy areas or other considerations

**1. Stigma and Cultural Barriers**

*Challenge:* Persistent stigma around addiction creates reluctance to engage with recovery services, particularly among Hispanic/Latino communities and student athletes who fear reputation damage.

*Impact:* Initial CRC recruitment at other universities showed 40% lower participation than projected due to stigma concerns.

*Solutions Implemented:*
- Developed "wellness" focused messaging that destigmatizes recovery
- Recruited diverse peer specialists who share cultural backgrounds
- Created anonymous entry points through QR codes and online resources
- Partner with respected campus organizations to normalize participation

**2. Training No-Show Challenge**

*Challenge:* Despite confirmed registrations, we experience significant no-shows for peer training. In our May 2025 cohort, 57 confirmed participants resulted in only 35 showing up the first week, with 30 completing the program.

*Impact:* 39% no-show rate affects resource planning and reduces overall program impact. Many registrants face barriers including housing instability, limited internet access, and reliance on borrowed devices or library computers.

*Solutions Implemented:*
- Created Peer Community Navigator intern position to follow up with all no-shows
- Send 7 email reminders before training starts
- Partner with recovery groups to provide laptop access
- Maintain accessible schedule (Tuesdays/Thursdays 5-7pm MST) accommodating work schedules
- Emphasize free training value while maintaining commitment to ACCESS over deposits

**3. Sustained Engagement Challenges**

*Challenge:* Maintaining consistent participation across academic calendar with breaks, finals, and competing priorities. Stampede events face similar challenges with attendee follow-through.

*Impact:* 35% drop-off between event attendance and ongoing service engagement.

*Solutions Implemented:*
- Hired dedicated student navigator to maintain contact
- Developed SAFE App for continuous connection
- Created incentive programs including academic credit opportunities
- Established multiple engagement touchpoints beyond events

**4. Rural Access and Transportation**

*Challenge:* Weld County's vast geographic area (4,000 square miles) creates significant barriers for rural residents accessing services concentrated in Greeley.

- Transportation vouchers for training programs

**5. Workforce Development Pipeline**
