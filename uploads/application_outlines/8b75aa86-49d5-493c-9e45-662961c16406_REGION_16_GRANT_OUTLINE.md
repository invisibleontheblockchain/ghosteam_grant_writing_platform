## PEER SPECIALIST TRAINING PROGRAM

### English Language Cohorts
- **Total to Train**: 20 El Paso County Certified Family Peer Specialists
- **Number of Cohorts**: 5 English-language cohorts
- **Lead Facilitator**: Darian Elmendorf
- **Format**: 8 weeks, Tuesdays & Thursdays, 5-7pm MST
- **Total Hours**: 60 hours over 8 weeks

### Spanish Language Cohorts  
- **Total to Train**: 10 El Paso County Certified Family Peer Specialists
- **Number of Cohorts**: 2 Spanish-language cohorts
- **Lead Facilitator**: Jennifer Vazquez
- **Format**: 100% Spanish (all live sessions, course materials, career pathways)
- **Timeline**: 1 cohort October 2025, 2 cohorts in 2026

### Training Background
- COPA-approved Peer Recovery Specialist Training program
- Training partner: Polaris Pathways
- Currently training 240+ peers across Colorado:
  - 80 peers in Denver County (2025-26)
  - 80 peers in Mesa County (2025-26)
  - 80 peers in Boulder County (2025-26)
  - 20 peers in Weld County (2025)
  - 20 peers in Adams County (2026)

### Training Challenges & Solutions
- Issue: 57 confirmed → only 35 showed up in last cohort
- Solution: Peer Community Navigator intern follows up with no-shows
- Access priority: Free training to ensure accessibility
- Many participants face housing instability, internet access issues
- Participants use library computers, borrowed laptops from recovery groups

## CORE PROGRAM COMPONENTS

### 1. Peer Workforce Development
- **Goal**: Grow certified peer workforce in El Paso County
- **Target**: 30 total peers (20 English + 10 Spanish)
- **Strategy**: Leverage SAFE's proven training model from other counties
- **Partners**: Treatment centers, RCOs, community organizations

### 2. Sober Support Tents at Events
**Services Provided**:
- Recovery support and peer connections
- Harm reduction education
- Naloxone kit distribution and training
- Resource navigation and referrals
- 4 certified peers per event earning certification hours (toward 500 required)

### 3. Student/Intern Position
- **Type**: Paid El Paso County student receiving internship credit
- **Responsibilities**:
  - Build relationships with local treatment/recovery organizations
  - Coordinate ticket distribution (5 tickets per new partner organization)
  - Follow up with peer training no-shows
  - Support event coordination and data collection

### 4. SAFE App Data System
**Features**:
- Streamlined attendee information collection
- Peer interaction tracking
- Resource sharing hub (vetted partners and services)
- Event signup for peers and community
- Real-time program feedback
- Examples: Jefferson County LEAP program shared by peers

## JENNIFER VAZQUEZ BIO

Jennifer Vazquez serves as SAFE's Peer Facilitator & Program Director. Hired by WCAHEC in May 2023 to run a Federal Department of Labor grant (completing February 2025), she brings extensive bilingual public health experience. 

Since September 2023, Jennifer has taught the Not Prescribed and Opioid Awareness class multiple times. Her bilingual expertise has expanded SAFE's reach to the Latino Chamber of Commerce and other outlets.

Jennifer is certified by "Bridging the Gap," completing their 40-hour interpretation course in 2020. She has provided translation services at Lamar and Mesa County Health Departments and WCAHEC. Raised in a bilingual household, she continues supporting her language and culture through family and community engagement.

## PROGRAM PHILOSOPHY

### Access First
- No financial barriers to training
- Support for those with housing instability
- Flexible attendance for working participants
- Library computer access for those without technology

### Quality Over Speed
- 8-week training model (not compressed 1-2 weeks)
- Time for reflection and integration
- Building genuine peer connections
- 60 hours allows proper skill development

### Community-Driven
- Peers contribute resources (LEAP program example)
- Vetted partner organizations
- Local supervisor standards maintained
- Cross-county volunteer opportunities
