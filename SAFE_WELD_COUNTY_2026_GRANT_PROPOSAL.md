**Submitted by**: SAFE (Sober Activities for Everyone)

**Project Period**: January 1, 2026 - December 31, 2026  
**Date Submitted**: August 1, 2025

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

SAFE (Sober Activities for Everyone) requests $175,000 from the Weld Regional Opioid Council to expand bilingual peer specialist training and community recovery support in Weld County. This 12-month initiative will train 40 certified Family Peer Specialists (20 English, 20 Spanish cohorts), conduct 12 community sober support events, and reach 800+ Weld County residents with evidence-based recovery programming.

Weld County faces critical gaps in Spanish-language recovery services and certified peer specialist workforce capacity. Our proven model addresses these needs through COPA-approved training, technology-enhanced support via the SAFE App platform, and high-visibility community events that reduce stigma while providing immediate resource navigation. With documented 82.76% participant satisfaction rates and successful operations across 6 Colorado counties, SAFE uniquely combines cultural responsiveness, quality training standards, and innovative community engagement strategies.

Expected outcomes include 75%+ training completion rates, 80%+ improvement in recovery resource awareness, establishment of sustainable peer specialist workforce pipeline, and measurable stigma reduction through positive recovery visibility at major community gatherings.

### Existing Weld County Service Gap Analysis

This project fills critical gaps identified in Weld County's recovery infrastructure:

**Gap 1 - Spanish-Language Peer Support Services**: Currently no comprehensive Spanish-language peer specialist training programs exist in Weld County despite Latino residents comprising significant population percentage. SAFE provides 100% Spanish curriculum delivery including cultural adaptation and traditional healing practice integration.

**Gap 2 - Certified Peer Specialist Workforce Shortage**: Weld County healthcare systems report difficulty recruiting certified peer specialists. Our training pipeline directly addresses this shortage while establishing employment partnerships.

**Gap 3 - Rural Community Access Barriers**: Geographic isolation and transportation challenges limit rural resident access to recovery services. SAFE's mobile app platform and community event model brings services directly to underserved areas.

**Gap 4 - Stigma Reduction Programming**: Limited positive recovery visibility at major community gatherings perpetuates stigma barriers. SAFE's community event strategy provides high-visibility sober support while normalizing recovery conversations.

**Gap 5 - Technology-Enhanced Peer Support**: Existing services lack integrated technology platforms for peer connectivity and resource sharing. SAFE App fills this gap with real-time engagement capabilities.
